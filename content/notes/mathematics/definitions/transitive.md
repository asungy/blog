## Definition
Let \(\rho\) be a relation on a set \(S\). We say that \(\rho\) is **transitive** if, whenever \(a \rho b\) and \(b \rho c\), we also have \(a \rho c\). 

## Example
On \(\mathbb{Z}\), the relations \(\leq\) and \(\lt\) are both transitive.
